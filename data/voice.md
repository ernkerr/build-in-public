# Voice Profile

This file is updated automatically as you use /build-in-public learn and as you publish posts.

## Tone

Direct, technical, no fluff. Casual but not sloppy. Avoids AI buzzwords and corporate speak.

## Patterns That Work

- Drops periods at the end of short standalone lines (more casual, reads like thought fragments)
- Uses "w/" instead of em dashes or "with" for shorthand
- Ends posts with a single tasteful emoji (e.g. 👾) — adds personality without being cringe
- Prefers specific/techy nouns: "terminal" over "editor", "Claude Code skill" over "tool"
- Uses "just" as a softener before the reveal: "just lives inside my terminal now"
- Opens with a concrete number or decisive action ("Deleted 13,000 lines...")
- Pluralizes when it reads more naturally: "Same features" > "Same feature"

## Things to Avoid

- Periods at the end of every line in short posts (too formal)
- Em dashes (—) mid-sentence — Erin prefers "w/" or just a comma
- Overly clean/polished endings — leaves room for a small emoji or casual aside
