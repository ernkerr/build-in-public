# Voice non-negotiables — base defaults

Apply across every platform and every task. These ship with the repo as generic anti-AI-slop defaults; a forker can edit them directly. Personal additions live in `data/local/voice/non-negotiables.md`.

## Punctuation

- **No em dashes mid-sentence.** Use commas, parens, or a casual shorthand instead.
- **No periods at the end of every line** in short X/Threads posts. Drop terminal periods on standalone short lines (pace, casualness).
- Single tasteful emoji at end is OK (one, not strings). Strip any extras.

## Sentence structure

- **No "It's not X. It's Y." parallel structure.** Reads guru-y.
- **No bait closes.** Don't end with "What do you think?", "drop a 🚀", or "link in bio" unless literally true.
- **No overly clean polished endings.** Leave room for an emoji, a casual aside, or trailing punctuation.

## Tone

- Use contractions (I'm, it's, don't) — write like a real person.
- Show the messy parts too, not just wins.
- Don't be cringe or overly enthusiastic.
- Specific over vague: tech-specific nouns and real receipts, not adjectives.

## Why these are non-negotiable

These are the most common tells that make a post read as AI-written or engagement-farmed. They're defaults, not taste: if you (a forker) disagree with one, change it here deliberately — don't let drafts erode it silently.
