# Voice — Facebook (Page) — base defaults

## Audience

Friends, family, casual followers, people who knew you before you were a builder. Not peers/recruiters (that's LinkedIn). Not strangers-on-the-feed (that's X). The audience already cares about you; they don't need the credential setup.

## Rhythm

Conversational. Like telling a friend over coffee what you've been up to. Longer than X, less essay-shaped than LinkedIn. Paragraphs can wander a little.

## Format constraints

- No hard character limit, but ~500-1500 chars is the sweet spot for the FB feed. Longer is OK if the story earns it.
- Line breaks for breathing room (FB collapses walls of text).
- One photo or screenshot lifts engagement a lot here. Mention if there's one.

## Voice direction

- Open with the human, not the headline. ("I've been working on this thing for weeks..." > "Today I shipped...")
- Plain-language the tech. Say "the AI thing that writes posts" not "the LLM-powered drafting agent." Friends shouldn't need a glossary.
- Show feeling more than on LinkedIn. Excited, frustrated, tired, weirdly proud — those land here.
- Personal context is welcome. "Spent the weekend at my parents' and..." style intros are fine.
- No hashtags. FB hashtags read as performative; they're for someone else's algorithm.
- No "build-in-public" language as a meta-frame. The friends-and-family audience doesn't need to know it's a movement.

## What translates from LinkedIn → FB

Take the LinkedIn version, strip:
- Hashtags
- "Thoughts?" / "Drop a comment" / engagement bait
- Industry jargon ("LLM", "TAM", "OSS maintainer", "SaaS", "stack")
- The implied peer audience ("as builders, we know...")

Add:
- A first-person opener that lands in your life (where you were, how you felt)
- Plain-language replacements for any technical noun
- Optional: one specific human detail (a thing your kid said, a meal you ate while debugging, the weather)

## CTA rules

- Default: no CTA.
- Allowed: a real invitation if relevant ("anyone in SF want to grab coffee?", "if you want to try it, here's the link") — never engagement bait.
- Banned: rhetorical questions, "share if you agree," anything that feels like recruiting.
