# Voice — Instagram → Hook — base defaults

The first 1-2 seconds. The scroll-stopper. Visual + audio + text overlay all working together.

## Direction

- Visual must change within 0.5s of frame 1 (motion or cut, not a static intro card).
- Audio: drop into the most arresting moment, not a slow build.
- Text overlay carries the claim or question that justifies watching the rest.
- Hook STRUCTURE comes from a formula below; personal voice/tone is layered on top. The formula does the stopping; the voice does the staying.

## Hook formulas (pick one per reel)

1. **Pattern interrupt** — open with something weird, wrong, or surprising. Visual or verbal. Breaks the scroll because the brain has to resolve "wait, what is that?"
2. **Open loop** — start with a question that can't be answered without watching to the end. The answer must actually arrive in the reel, not get withheld.
3. **Specific over vague** — "3:47am" not "late night," "1,524 commits" not "lots of commits." Specificity is credibility and texture.
4. **Social proof framing** — "People who [do X] always [do Y]." Viewer has to keep watching to find out if they're one of them.
5. **Numbered list psychology** — "3 things I…" / "5 mistakes I made…" The brain treats it as a finish line and won't stop until the list completes.
6. **Call out your person** — "If you're a [solo dev / designer / etc.], this is for you." Explicit audience targeting filters viewers in fast.
7. **Discovery / surprise** — "I just found out [unexpected fact]." Creates an open loop (what did you find out?) + intimacy (you're being let in on something).
8. **Things about me: [list]** — list-of-personal-facts hook. Works for personal storytelling, "get to know me" content, anything autobiographical. Stacks naturally with numbered-list psychology.
9. **Show-the-object** — "This is my [object]. It's [adjective], [adjective], [adjective]." Pattern interrupt (holding up an item) + specific over vague (the adjectives). Pairs with a held-shot of the object. For projects: "This is [project name]. It's a [adjective], [adjective] [thing that does X]."

Mix-and-match is fine (e.g., pattern interrupt visual + open loop line). Don't stack more than 2 — gets noisy.
