### Structural notes (reel + caption combined)

The **reel and caption use different hook strategies** — worth understanding before borrowing:

| Beat | Reel transcript | Caption |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | Open-loop question: "what if your morning journal could turn into visual art" | Obsession statement: "i've been obsessed with how typography and design can articulate emotion and intent..." |
| Body | List of inputs (punctuation, case, rhythm, tone, energy) → output (custom wallpaper) | Same list, but compressed to one sentence |
| Story | "the funny part is I built most of this while sitting on a rock in Central Park" + Cursor Cloud Agents narrative | Skipped entirely in caption |
| Close | Punchy phrase: "code and touch grass at the same time" → CTA | Just the CTA + App Store P.S. |
| CTA | "comment journal and I'll send it to you" | "comment 'journal' if you wanna try out the app I built :)" |

**Key beats:**
- **Reel hook is an open-loop question, not an obsession statement.** Question creates a "I have to know" pull. (Pattern #2 from hook formulas.)
- **The middle has a making-of story, not just a product description.** "I built it on a rock in Central Park" is concrete + unexpected. Specific over vague.
- **The tool plug is woven as a sub-story, not a tag.** Cursor isn't mentioned, it's *demonstrated* through the rock-in-Central-Park narrative (cloud agents → phone → builds running while she's outside).
- **Punchy phrase before CTA** — "code and touch grass at the same time" is the line that gets quoted. Earns the CTA.
- **Lists are read fast as on-screen rhythm.** "punctuation / case choices / sentence rhythm / emotional tone / energy behind your words" — 5 quick beats, builds momentum.

**Reel length:** ~45-50s based on pacing. Longer than Erin's 30s target — so the ascii-cam reel will need to compress at least one section.

### What to borrow for ascii-cam

- **Open-loop hook OR obsession hook** — both work; pick one. (Erin already leans obsession: "I've always loved ASCII.")
- **Inputs → outputs rhythm in the body** — fast list of what goes in, what comes out. For ascii-cam: face / lighting / motion → ASCII portrait in real time.
- **Origin story as the middle, not just a tag.** Pink Sunset story IS the equivalent of "rock in Central Park" — concrete moment that produced the idea.
- **Punchy close phrase before CTA.** Equivalent for ascii-cam: TBD (the "ascii vision" regret callback might be the move).
- **Comment-word CTA** — single word, candidates: "ascii", "cam", "vision".
- **All-lowercase, casual punctuation** in the caption.

### What to skip

- **#ad and #CursorCreator** — Erin's reel is NOT a paid partnership. Tag @cursor and @claude organically, but no #ad disclosure and no creator-program hashtag.
- App Store P.S. — ascii-cam is web-only and already live, no logistical disclaimer needed.

## Cross-ref synthesis

What's consistent across all 4 references:

### Hooks
- **POV: framing** (Refs #2, #4) — strong pattern interrupt + audience targeting in 4 words.
- **Open-loop question** (Ref #1 reel) — "what if [X]?"
- **Obsession opener** (Ref #1 caption) — "i've been obsessed with…"
- **Direct statement** (Ref #3) — "I built a website to [do X]" — works when *X* is curious on its own.

Erin already ruled out "what if X" framing for the ascii-cam reel — so we're choosing between **POV:** or **direct statement / obsession opener.**

### Body structure
- Most reels (Refs #1, #3) have a **personal-motivation middle**: rock in Central Park, walked past a statue, wanted to get outside. Specific place + specific moment.
- Ref #2 skips the story (it's seasonal so it doesn't need one).
- Ref #4 replaces story with step-by-step process visuals.

### Tool integration
- Always tagged, never the subject.
- Best when it's woven into the story ("I asked Claude," "Cursor Cloud Agents handled it from my phone") rather than dropped at the end.
- Ref #3 is the cleanest example: tool appears mid-narrative, briefly, then gets out of the way.

### Captions
- All lowercase or mostly lowercase.
- Short — one to three lines, usually one.
- Quirky punctuation: "tmrrrrr," "~," "hehe," ":)", ":-)", "🕺🪩"
- No hard CTA in 3 of 4. Hard CTAs ("comment journal") only appeared in the paid #ad post.
- Tool tagged with @ handle.
- Hashtags optional; vary widely.

### CTAs
- Hard "comment X" CTAs are an *ad* move, not an organic move.
- Organic posts use **URL + soft aside** ("littlecalendars.com (i made a little advent calendar app hehe)") OR **no CTA at all** (Refs #3, #4).
- For ascii-cam: lean soft. Drop ascii-cam.com + a parenthetical aside. Maybe a comment-word CTA if Erin really wants engagement, but it's optional.

## Ref #5 — Wacomo (pen system, personal storytelling)

**Saved:** 2026-05-19
**Format:** Voiceover reel, conversational run-on, very loose pacing
**Content type:** **NOT** "posting a project" — this is personal storytelling / "things about me" / lifestyle. Saved because Erin loves the style. Worth a separate format slot in the future.

### Transcript

> This is my favorite pen, it's green, swiss made, and it writes pretty nice
>
> The problem is that I have a growing collection of other pens that I also love very much but now that I'm no longer in school it feels like the reasons for using a pen are decreasing while my love for stationary and the number of pens I have keeps increasing
>
> this I imagine is hugely upsetting for most of my pens because they're locked in a dark pencil case with nothing to do
>
> so I've come up with a system that I think will improve the situation
>
> there's 2 rules
>
> number 1 if I don't need technology to do something I should do it with a pen and paper to do lists feelings brainstorms you get the idea
>
> and the second rule is that every pen must have its own unique job
>
> for example my Pentel sign 230 is the draw yourself pen and it lives right here or my LAMY Safari fountain pen, thats the feelings pen because it's smooth and fast or my penten 120 automatic that ones for quick notes
>
> I'd tell you more about my pens but I make short videos and I don't have that much time left

### Per-ref notes

- **Hook = show-the-object + adjective list.** "This is my favorite pen, it's green, swiss made, and it writes pretty nice." Visual is the pen in hand. Already established as hook formula #9 in `hook.md`.
- **Structure: problem → system → examples → meta close.** Identifies a tension ("reasons for using a pen are decreasing while my love for stationary keeps increasing"), proposes a solution ("a system… 2 rules"), gives specific examples (Pentel sign 230, LAMY Safari, penten 120), and closes with a self-referential joke about the format itself ("I make short videos and I don't have that much time left").
- **Tone = run-on conversational.** Almost no commas where you'd expect them. Reads like one continuous thought. The lack of punctuation IS the personality.
- **Self-aware aside as personality**: "this I imagine is hugely upsetting for most of my pens because they're locked in a dark pencil case with nothing to do" — anthropomorphizes the pens, adds warmth.
- **Meta close is the kicker.** "I'd tell you more about my pens but I make short videos and I don't have that much time left." Doesn't pretend to wrap up neatly — admits the format constraint, becomes part of the joke.

### What's relevant for ascii-cam (and future personal reels)

- The "show-the-object" hook structure works for projects too: "This is ascii-cam, the website I built that turns your webcam into live ASCII art."
- Run-on conversational pacing is a stylistic option for the explainer beat — less polished, more like a friend explaining over coffee.
- Self-referential close as alternative to a kicker punchline.
- **Specific model numbers / version numbers** = credibility texture. Equivalent for ascii-cam: "120 by 70 grid," "30 frames a second," real specs.
